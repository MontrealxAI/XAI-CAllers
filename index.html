Qualify Who Picked Up

📱 Owner Answers Directly
They say "speaking" or give their name. Skip straight to Step 3 — The Opener.
2
Gatekeeper Dialogue Options
Only if they said NO above — pick the column that matches who answered
🧠 Mindset for this step
Don't fight them. Don't trick them. Be so calm and natural that transferring you is easier than explaining why not. You're a peer calling a peer — not a salesperson asking for access.
📋 Receptionist / Front Desk
"Who's the owner there?"
Get the name first. The second they give it, follow with: "Is [Name] around?" — now you sound like you already know them.
→ If they ask "what's it regarding?"
"It's about their phone system — just a quick question for them directly."
Vague enough to pass. Honest enough to sleep at night. Never say "sales call."
→ If they push harder
"Totally fair — I help [trade] businesses capture missed calls. 30 seconds. Is there a better time to reach them directly?"
Now you're asking for a time, not a transfer. Same result either way.
📞 Call Centre / Dispatcher
"Who handles decisions about how the business manages its incoming calls — is that the owner or more of an ops person?"
Qualify who to talk to before asking to be transferred blindly.
→ If they say "that's me"
"Perfect — when calls come in after-hours or when everyone's busy, what happens to those right now?"
They may be your actual contact. Run the opener from here — go to Step 3.
→ If they say "that's the owner"
"Got it — what's the best way to reach them directly? I'd rather not keep going through the main line."
Ask for a direct number or email. Being straight with them earns a little goodwill.
🔁 Can't Get Through At All
"Is there a direct line or email for the owner? I'd rather not leave a general voicemail."
Most receptionists will give you an email at minimum. An email is a win.
→ If they won't give anything
"No worries — when do you usually see them in the office? I'll try back then."
Get the window. Call back at exactly that time: "The person who answered mentioned this was a good time to catch [Name]."
→ Last resort
Call before 8am or after 5pm. Owners answer their own phones before the gatekeeper arrives.
Works especially well Monday and Friday. Trades start early.

3
The Opener
You're talking to the owner. Start here.
📞 Say this — word for word
"Hey, quick question — when you guys are out on jobs or after-hours, do calls ever slip through?"
Smile slightly. Slow. Downward inflection at the end. Then stop. Silence is your friend — let them fill it.
4
Branch on Their Answer
Listen first — then pick your column
✅ They Say YES / Some
"When that happens — is it mostly voicemail, or do some just disappear completely?"
Shut up. Let them talk. Their answer is your pitch.
→ Then ask
"If those calls were captured automatically and booked in instantly — would that be useful?"
Simple yes or no. Don't elaborate. Wait.
→ If yes — close immediately
"Perfect. Let me show you in 10 minutes. Tomorrow afternoon or Thursday morning?"
Two specific times. Never "when works for you?"
✗ They Say NO / We Don't Miss Calls
"Nice — are you running a live answering service, or is it handled internally?"
Find the crack. There's always one.
→ If internally
"Got it — when someone's tied up on a job and a new call comes in, what actually happens to that call?"
Let them explain. They'll usually reveal the gap themselves.
→ If answering service
"Are they booking directly into your calendar, or just forwarding messages for you to follow up?"
Message forwarding is not a booked job. The delay is the friction.
→ Once friction found
"That's exactly what this fixes. Worth 10 minutes? Tomorrow or Thursday?"
📩 They Say Send Info
"I can — but it makes way more sense if you hear it in action. It's 10 minutes."
Agree, then redirect. Don't argue.
→ Immediately pivot to time
"What's better — tomorrow afternoon or Thursday morning?"
→ If they still insist on email
Send it. Follow up in 2 days:
"Sent that over Tuesday — did it make sense? Two minutes."
5
Handle Objections
Every single response ends with a time close
They SayYou Say
"We don't get many calls." "Two possibilities — either people aren't finding you online yet, which we can help with. Or you're getting more than you realize and missing them. Do you actually have a way to track how many go unanswered?"
→ Creates doubt. Opens the door. No tracking = your opening.
"How much does it cost?" "Starts at $249 a month. Most guys find one recovered job covers the whole month — that's the point. Worth 10 minutes to see if it makes sense for you?"
→ Never defend the price. Anchor against one job value. Back to the time close.
"I already have someone answering." "That's great — are they available nights and weekends too? And when they're not, what's the backup?"
→ Nobody has 24/7 human coverage. Find where it ends.
"My customers won't like AI." "Most people say that before they hear it. Call our demo line right now and tell me if you'd know the difference — 60 seconds. 438-886-3878."
→ Don't argue. Let the AI sell itself.
"Need to think / talk to my partner." "Makes sense — when are you two usually together? I can do a quick call with both of you, easier than phone tag."
→ Include the decision-maker. Never let "think about it" end without a next step booked.
"Too busy / bad time." "Totally — when's a better time this week? I only need 10 minutes."
→ Don't apologize. Get a specific alternate time. "Next month" = calendar it exactly and call then.
6
Close & Confirm the Demo
The second they agree — lock it in before they change their mind
📅 Time Close — Use Every Single Time
"Are you more free tomorrow afternoon or Thursday morning?"
Two options only. Never open-ended. If both are bad, ask what works — then hold the new time.
✅ Once They Pick a Time
"Perfect — I'll send a calendar invite right now. All you need is 10 minutes and a phone. Sound good?"
Send the invite the second you hang up. Include 438-886-3878. Text morning of: "Looking forward to the call at [time]. Demo line if curious now: 438-886-3878"

Under 20 seconds. No pitch. One goal: get a callback.
🔴 Leave This
"Hey [Name], [Your name] from Montreal X AI — quick question about missed calls. Give me a ring when you get a sec. [Your number]. Talk soon."
Curious tone only. No product name. No pitch. You want a callback — that's it.
📱 Text 30 Min After

Short. No context. The curiosity gap does the work. Don't explain it — let them call back to find out.

Calling Schedule
When to dial — and why each window works
🔥 Best Windows
8:00–9:30am & 4:00–6:00pm
Pre-job and end-of-day. Owners have their phones. Highest answer rate of the day. Hit cold leads here.
⚡ Good Windows
1:00–3:00pm
After lunch, between jobs. Good for callbacks and warm leads. Lower pickup on cold outreach.
🚫 Avoid for Cold
10:00am–12:00pm
On a job, hands dirty, can't talk. Only call this window if they specifically told you to.
🔥 Morning Window
Cold calls only — highest answer rate of the day.
You solo. Trades are pre-job, phone in hand. Hit new leads hard.
⚡ Afternoon Window
Both on — demos, warm callbacks, cold outreach.
Partner jumps in here. Handoff flagged leads from morning. Tag-team.
🔥 Evening Window
Both dialing together — best window of the day.
Trades wrapping jobs, in the truck. Cold + callbacks. Go hard.
