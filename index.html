⚡ The 4 Unbreakable Rules

Always close on a time — never "I'll send info"

Every answer leads to a next question or a time ask
Step 1 — The Opener
📞 Say This — Word For Word
"Hey, quick question — when you guys are out on jobs or after-hours, do calls ever slip through?"
Smile slightly. Speak slowly. Downward inflection at the end. Then — stop talking. Silence is your friend.
Step 2 — Listen & Branch Follow their answer
✓ They Say YES / Some
"Got it. When that happens — is it mostly going to voicemail, or do you think some just disappear completely?"
Shut up. Let them talk. Nod. Their answer is your pitch.
→ Then ask
"If those calls were captured automatically and booked in instantly — would that actually be useful for you?"
Simple yes/no. Don't elaborate. Wait.
→ If yes, close immediately
🗓 Time Close
"Perfect. Let me show you exactly how it works — takes 10 minutes. Are you more free tomorrow afternoon or Thursday morning?"
✗ They Say NO / We Don't Miss Calls
"Nice — are you running a live answering service, or is it handled internally?"
Find the crack. There's always one.
→ If internally
"Got it — so when someone's tied up on a job and a new call comes in, what actually happens to that call?"
Let them explain. They'll usually reveal the gap themselves.
→ If answering service
"Are they booking directly into your calendar — or are they just forwarding messages and you follow up?"
Message forwarding ≠ booked job. Find the delay = find the friction.
→ Once friction found
🗓 Time Close
"That's exactly what this fixes. Worth 10 minutes? Tomorrow or Thursday?"
📩 They Say Send Info
"I can do that — but honestly it'll make a lot more sense if you hear it in action. It's literally a 10-minute walkthrough."
Don't argue. Agree, then redirect.
→ Immediately pivot to time
"What's better for you — tomorrow afternoon or Thursday morning?"
If they still push for email only, get their email and send a demo recording. Don't ghost. Follow up in 2 days.
→ After sending info (follow up)
"Hey — I sent that over Tuesday. Did you get a chance to look? Two minutes — did it make sense?"
Short. No apology for following up. Downward inflection.
Step 3 — Common Objections & Responses
Them"We don't really get a lot of calls."
You "Totally fair — two possibilities there. Either people aren't finding you online yet, which is something we can help with. Or you might be getting more than you realize and missing them. Do you have a way to actually track how many go unanswered?"
→ Creates doubt. Opens door. If they admit no tracking = your opening.
Them"We're too busy right now / not a good time."
You "Totally get it — when's a better time this week? I just need 10 minutes, and I can work around your schedule."
→ Don't apologize. Ask for a specific alternate time. If they say "try next month" — calendar it and call exactly then.
Them"How much does it cost?"
You "Depends on what you need — starts at $249 a month. Most guys find one recovered job covers the whole month. That's kind of the point. Worth 10 minutes to see if it makes sense for you?"
→ Don't defend the price. Anchor it against one job value. Then immediately back to the time close.
Them"I already have someone answering calls."
You "That's great — are they available nights and weekends too? And when they're not, what's the backup?"
→ Almost nobody has 24/7 human coverage. That's your gap. Don't argue with their current setup — find where it ends.
Them"Is it AI? My customers won't like that."
You "Completely understand — most people say that before they hear it. It's not a robot voice. You can call our demo line right now and tell me if you'd know the difference. Takes 60 seconds."
→ Don't argue. Give them a live experience. Hand them the number: 438-886-3878.
Them"I need to talk to my partner / think about it."
You "Makes sense. When are you two usually in the same place? I can do a quick 10-minute call with both of you — easier than playing phone tag."
→ Offer to include the decision-maker directly. Never let "think about it" be the final answer without a next step.
Voicemail Script
